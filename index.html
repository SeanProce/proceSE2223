Opening Bottles For the Disabled

By Sean Proce

Background

The purpose of this study was to design, build, and test a device that would allow the disbaled to reduce the time it takes to open water bottles with one hand. A prototype was designed using 3D modeling on Fusion 360 combined with 3D printing. The prototype consisted of several pieces that would be constructed into the final device. The device used a threaded rod to twist until a tight grip formed with the bottle cap. Then the bottle could be twisted until the cap came loose. Testing consisted of a group of four participants who each opened 10 bottles. Five of these were done without using the device, and five were done while using it. The times for each bottle were analyzed to determine if the product made a significant decrease in the time it took to open a bottle with one hand. The results showed that there was a significant decrease, and the device greatly reduced the time required for a one-armed person to open a bottle.

Rationale

Twistable caps are a design commonly used in water bottles and other drinks. A typical consumer will open this bottle by gripping the bottle with one hand and turning the cap with the other hand. However, many people have lost fingers, hands, or arms in accidents or to disease. This issue can make it difficult to open one of these bottles. Therefore, people with only one hand need a tool to aid them in opening and closing twistable bottles.
